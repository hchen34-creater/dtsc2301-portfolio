About Me

My name is Haoran Chen and I am taking data science at UNC Charlotte. Before these classes, I thought data science was mostly about writing code and making charts. It turned out to be a lot more complicated and way more interesting than I expected.

I am also in the Army as a reservist, which shapes how I think about problem solving: start with the actual problem before jumping to solutions. That mindset transfers pretty directly to data science.

I am drawn to questions about health, behavior, education, and inequality because those are the topics where the data actually affects real people.

Why This Matters

When people talk about data science, it is easy to imagine something purely technical: code, charts, predictions, and dashboards. The reason I am excited to learn it is that many of the questions I care about are social questions.

Education, health, employment, housing, and public policy are all shaped by human decisions and systems. Data science gives us tools to look at patterns across a community instead of only seeing individual cases.

At the same time, modeling social topics and people comes with real responsibility. People are not just rows in a spreadsheet, and data is never a perfect mirror of reality. What we measure, how we collect it, and who gets included all matter.
