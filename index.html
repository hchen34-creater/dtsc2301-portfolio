About Me

My name is Haoran Chen and I am taking data science at UNC Charlotte. Before these classes, I thought data science was mostly about writing code and making charts. It turned out to be more complicated and more interesting than I expected.

I am also in the Army as a reservist, which shapes how I think about problem solving: start with the actual problem before jumping to solutions. That mindset transfers directly to data science.

I am drawn to questions about health, behavior, education, and inequality because those are topics where the data affects real people. My goal is to communicate results clearly while being honest about limitations and uncertainty.

Why This Matters

When people talk about data science, it is easy to imagine something purely technical: code, charts, predictions, and dashboards. The reason I am interested in data science is that many of the questions I care about are social questions.

Education, health, employment, housing, and public policy are all shaped by human decisions and systems. Data science gives us tools to look at patterns across a community instead of only seeing individual cases.

At the same time, modeling social topics and people comes with responsibility. People are not just rows in a spreadsheet. What we measure, how we collect it, and who gets included all matter.
